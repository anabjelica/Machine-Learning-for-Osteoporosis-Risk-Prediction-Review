# Machine-Learning-for-Osteoporosis-Risk-Prediction-Review
# Overview:
# Osteoporosis is a multifactorial skeletal disease marked by low bone mineral density, disrupted bone remodeling, and increased fracture risk. Although current diagnostic methods and clinical risk prediction tools are widely available, they often fail to capture the biological, genetic, and structural factors that influence bone quality and fracture susceptibility.
# This literature review examines machine learning approaches for osteoporosis and fracture risk prediction through the integration of clinical risk factors, medical imaging, and biological biomarkers. It evaluates supervised and deep learning models, multimodal predictive strategies, and the limitations that currently hinder clinical implementation.
